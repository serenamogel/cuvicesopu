Biography

Paul Engle was born in Iowa on October 12, 1908. Poet, author, editor, and teacher who helped to establish the superb reputation of the Iowa Writers’ Workshop. His best-known poetic works include Break the Heart’s Anger, Corn, and The Word of Love. He taught Philip Levine at the Iowa Writers’ Workshop. He was born in Cedar Rapids, Iowa, and received a Rhodes Scholarship to attend Oxford University. Worn Earth, his debut poetry collection, won the Yale Series of Younger Poets Competition.
